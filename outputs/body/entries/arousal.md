# Arousal

**1. The Hydraulic Problem:** Blood is the mechanism. Nitric oxide released in nerve endings relaxes smooth muscle in arterial walls. Blood floods in faster than veins can drain it away. Tissue swells with pressure — the penis can reach ten times its flaccid volume, the clitoris doubles. The body solves the engineering challenge of rigidity through fluid dynamics, turning spongy tissue into something load-bearing. Heart rate climbs. Blood pressure rises. The cardiovascular system redirects resources like a city rerouting traffic. This is arousal's most visible machinery, but the mechanism begins elsewhere.

**2. The Spinal Override:** Arousal can bypass the brain entirely. Paraplegics with complete spinal cord transections can still experience genital arousal and orgasm through reflex arcs that never reach consciousness. The sacral spinal cord receives sensory input and sends motor commands without consulting higher regions. This explains nocturnal erections during REM sleep, morning arousal, the phenomenon of arousal during sexual assault where conscious desire is absent. The body has its own ideas. The spine makes decisions the mind never authorized.

**3. The Victorian Cure:** Nineteenth-century physicians treated "female hysteria" with pelvic massage to induce "hysterical paroxysm" — orgasm by another name. The vibrator was invented in 1880s as an efficiency tool for doctors whose hands tired. Advertisements marketed it as a cure for headaches, wrinkles, asthma. Patients sought treatment regularly. No one acknowledged what was actually happening. The medical establishment prescribed arousal while denying its existence, and women returned weekly for their therapeutic convulsions, understanding perfectly what their doctors would not say.

**4. The Discordance:** Genital response and subjective desire don't always align. Studies using photoplethysmography show arousal patterns that contradict self-reported attraction. Women show genital arousal to images of bonobo intercourse. Men respond to stimuli they consciously find unappealing. The body prepares for scenarios the mind rejects. This arousal non-concordance appears adaptive — evolutionary insurance against injury during unwanted sex, perhaps, or simple vigilance. The genitals respond to sexual stimuli the way pupils respond to light: automatically, without consulting preference.

**5. The Invisible Catalogue:** Humans aroused by pregnancy. By amputated limbs. By being furniture. By watching others sneeze. By balloons popping. By the Eiffel Tower. Paraphilias number in the hundreds, each representing some crossed wire between sexual response and improbable trigger. Most develop before age ten. Most persist for life. The mechanisms remain mysterious — imprinting during development, unusual pattern recognition, random neural association hardened through repetition. The catalogue suggests human arousal is catastrophically flexible, capable of fusing to almost anything encountered during sexual development's critical window.

**6. The Asexual Testimony:** One percent of humans report no sexual attraction to anyone. Their bodies still respond to stimulation — arousal's hydraulics function normally — but the desire that precedes it, the appetite itself, is absent. They describe watching their body respond to touch the way someone might watch their knee jerk when tapped. This population reveals arousal as multiple systems that usually coordinate but need not. Genital response is one thing. Attraction is another. Desire is a third. The systems can operate independently, and in some bodies, some never activate at all.
