# Kill Criteria
***The list of deal-breakers that lets you say no fast***

> **Section:** Tools

**What it is:** A pre-decided list of conditions that automatically disqualify an option, no matter how attractive everything else looks. If an opportunity violates any item on your kill criteria list, you reject it immediately without further deliberation.

**When to use it:** When you're evaluating multiple opportunities (jobs, investments, projects, partnerships) and need to filter quickly. Especially valuable when you're prone to getting excited about new possibilities or when you face time pressure to decide.

**How to do it:** Before you start evaluating options, write down your non-negotiables — the things you absolutely won't accept. Be specific. "Bad culture" is too vague. "More than 50% travel" or "Reports to more than one person" works. Keep the list short — three to five criteria. These should be truly binary: either the option violates the criterion or it doesn't. Once your list exists, check every opportunity against it first, before you consider the upside.

**Example:** A software engineer job-hunting in 2021 wrote three kill criteria: no full-time remote option, stack includes PHP (a language she'd decided to move away from), or fewer than 10 engineers (she wanted to learn from a larger team). She received an offer from a startup with an exciting mission, strong equity package, and impressive founders. The engineering team had eight people. She turned it down in under an hour. Two years later, she's happy in her role at a 50-person company; the startup folded. The kill criteria let her avoid an agonizing deliberation about a fundamentally wrong-fit opportunity.

**Watch out for:** Kill criteria can become too rigid. Revisit them periodically — your circumstances change. Also resist the temptation to add criteria mid-evaluation to rationalize a decision you've already emotionally made. The power comes from deciding your deal-breakers before you fall in love with (or against) a specific option.
