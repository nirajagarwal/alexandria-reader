# The Amidah

The Amidah — "the Standing" — is the center of Jewish prayer, recited three times daily while standing, feet together, facing Jerusalem. It dates to the Second Temple period and was codified after the Temple's destruction in 70 CE. On weekdays it contains nineteen blessings; on Sabbaths and festivals, seven. Orthodox Jews recite it silently, then the leader repeats it aloud. Reform and Conservative communities often pray it together. The prayer moves through praise, petition, and thanksgiving — acknowledging God's power, asking for what is needed, expressing gratitude. It begins and ends with the same words used for two thousand years: Adonai, open my lips. Adonai, grant peace.

This is the opening of the weekday Amidah, the first three blessings — the section of pure praise that precedes all petition.

---

***Adonai, open my lips, that my mouth may declare Your praise.***

***Blessed are You, Adonai our God and God of our ancestors, God of Abraham, God of Isaac, and God of Jacob, the great, mighty, and awesome God, God supreme, who bestows loving kindness and creates all things, who remembers the loving deeds of our ancestors and will send a redeemer to their children's children for the sake of divine honor, with love.***

***Sovereign, Helper, Savior, Shield: Blessed are You, Adonai, Shield of Abraham.***

***You are mighty forever, Adonai. You give life to the dead; great is Your saving power. You sustain the living with loving kindness, give life to the dead with great mercy, support the falling, heal the sick, free the captive, and keep faith with those who sleep in the dust. Who is like You, Master of might, and who can compare to You, Sovereign who brings death and life and causes salvation to flourish?***

***You are faithful to give life to the dead. Blessed are You, Adonai, who gives life to the dead.***

***You are holy and Your name is holy, and holy ones praise You daily.***

***Blessed are You, Adonai, the holy God.***
