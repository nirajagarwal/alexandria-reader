# Kontakion for the Departed

This hymn belongs to the Byzantine funeral liturgy, sung at the graveside in the Orthodox Church. A kontakion is a form of liturgical poetry that emerged in the Eastern Christian tradition in the sixth century. This particular kontakion faces death directly — the body's return to dust, the soul's journey, the hope of resurrection. It is sung slowly, often antiphonally, the mourners standing around the open grave. The melody is ancient, sorrowful, and strangely steady. It does not flinch from what is happening. It names the truth of mortality and then makes a plea: remember this one, when You come into Your kingdom.

---

***All we go down to the dust;***
***and weeping o'er the grave we make our song:***
***Alleluia, Alleluia, Alleluia.***

***With the Saints give rest, O Christ,***
***to the soul of Thy servant,***
***where there is neither pain, nor sorrow, nor suffering,***
***but life everlasting.***

***Thou only art immortal,***
***who hast created and fashioned man.***
***For out of the earth were we mortals made,***
***and unto the earth shall we return again,***
***as Thou didst command when Thou madest me, saying:***
***For earth thou art, and unto earth shalt thou return.***
***Whither also all we mortals wend our way,***
***making of our funeral dirge the song:***
***Alleluia, Alleluia, Alleluia.***
