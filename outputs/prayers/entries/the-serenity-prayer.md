# The Serenity Prayer

Reinhold Niebuhr, the American theologian, is credited with composing this prayer in the early 1940s, though its origins remain somewhat uncertain. It gained wide circulation through Alcoholics Anonymous and other twelve-step programs, but its reach extends far beyond recovery communities. The prayer is typically spoken quietly, often at moments of decision or distress. It asks for three distinct gifts: serenity to accept what cannot be changed, courage to change what can be changed, and the wisdom to distinguish between the two. The prayer assumes that some things are genuinely beyond our power and that knowing the difference is itself a form of grace.

---

***God, grant me the serenity to accept the things I cannot change,***
***courage to change the things I can,***
***and wisdom to know the difference.***
