# The Metta Prayer

This prayer emerges from the Buddhist tradition, where *metta* means loving-kindness — a boundless goodwill toward all beings. It is practiced in meditation, spoken softly or held in silence, directing compassion first toward oneself, then outward in widening circles: to loved ones, to strangers, to those who cause harm, to all living things. The prayer does not ask for kindness to be given from elsewhere. It cultivates it within, then sends it forth. It is one of the oldest practices for training the heart toward universal compassion.

---

***May I be happy.***
***May I be well.***
***May I be safe.***
***May I be peaceful and at ease.***

***May you be happy.***
***May you be well.***
***May you be safe.***
***May you be peaceful and at ease.***

***May all beings be happy.***
***May all beings be well.***
***May all beings be safe.***
***May all beings be peaceful and at ease.***
