# Shehecheyanu

A Hebrew blessing of gratitude, spoken at moments of newness — the first fruit of the season, the lighting of festival candles, the putting on of new clothes, the arrival at a long-awaited day. It has been recited by Jews for centuries, a prayer that names the simple fact of having arrived: we have lived, we have been sustained, we have reached this time. Three verbs, each one a recognition. It is brief. It marks thresholds.

---

***Baruch atah Adonai, Eloheinu melech ha'olam,***
***shehecheyanu, v'kiy'manu, v'higiyanu laz'man hazeh.***

***Blessed are You, Lord our God, Ruler of the universe,***
***who has kept us alive, sustained us, and brought us to this season.***
