# Lighten Our Darkness

This collect appears in the Book of Common Prayer as part of Evening Prayer, said as darkness falls. It is ancient — variations exist from the early centuries of the church — and it has been prayed nightly in Anglican communities for five hundred years. The prayer names the danger that comes with darkness, both outward and inward, and asks for protection through the night. It is brief and direct: a request for defense, for keeping, for safe passage to morning.

---

***Lighten our darkness, we beseech thee, O Lord;***
***and by thy great mercy defend us***
***from all perils and dangers of this night;***
***for the love of thy only Son,***
***our Saviour Jesus Christ.***
***Amen.***
