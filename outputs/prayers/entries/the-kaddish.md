# The Kaddish

The Kaddish is an Aramaic prayer of praise recited in Jewish liturgy, most widely known in its form as the Mourner's Kaddish. It makes no mention of death. Instead, it magnifies and sanctifies God's name, asking that God's kingdom be established in the world. Mourners have recited it for centuries — daily for eleven months after a death, then annually on the yahrzeit. It is said standing, in the presence of a minyan, ten adult Jews gathered. The prayer binds the living to the dead through praise, not lament. It is among the oldest sustained uses of Aramaic in continuous liturgical practice.

---

***Yitgadal v'yitkadash sh'mei raba.***  
***Magnified and sanctified be God's great name.***

***B'alma di v'ra chirutei, v'yamlich malchutei***  
***In the world which God has created according to divine will, may God's kingdom be established***

***B'chayeichon uvyomeichon uvchayei d'chol beit Yisrael***  
***In your lifetime and in your days, and in the lifetime of all the house of Israel,***

***Ba'agala uvizman kariv, v'imru: Amen.***  
***Speedily and soon. And say: Amen.***

***Y'hei sh'mei raba m'varach l'alam ul'almei almaya.***  
***May God's great name be blessed forever and to all eternity.***

***Yitbarach v'yishtabach v'yitpa'ar v'yitromam v'yitnasei***  
***Blessed and praised, glorified and exalted, extolled and honored,***

***V'yit'hadar v'yit'aleh v'yit'halal sh'mei d'kud'sha b'rich hu.***  
***Magnified and lauded be the name of the Holy One, blessed be God.***

***L'eila min kol birchata v'shirata, tushb'chata v'nechemata***  
***Beyond all blessings and hymns, praises and consolations***

***Da'amiran b'alma, v'imru: Amen.***  
***That are uttered in the world. And say: Amen.***

***Y'hei sh'lama raba min sh'maya***  
***May there be abundant peace from heaven,***

***V'chayim aleinu v'al kol Yisrael, v'imru: Amen.***  
***And life for us and for all Israel. And say: Amen.***

***Oseh shalom bimromav, hu ya'aseh shalom aleinu***  
***May the One who makes peace in the high places make peace for us***

***V'al kol Yisrael, v'imru: Amen.***  
***And for all Israel. And say: Amen.***
