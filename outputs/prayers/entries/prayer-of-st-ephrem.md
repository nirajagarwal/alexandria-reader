# Prayer of St. Ephrem

Ephrem the Syrian, a fourth-century deacon and theologian, composed this prayer for use during Great Lent in the Eastern Christian tradition. It is prayed with prostrations — full bows to the ground — at specific moments, the body enacting what the words confess. The prayer names four sins that obscure the soul, then four virtues that might restore it. Orthodox Christians pray it daily through Lent, the prostrations marking each petition, the repetition pressing the prayer into muscle and bone. It reaches toward purity of heart, the clearing away of what blocks love.

---

***O Lord and Master of my life,***
***take from me the spirit of sloth, despair, lust of power, and idle talk.***

***But give rather the spirit of chastity, humility, patience, and love to Your servant.***

***Yea, O Lord and King,***
***grant me to see my own sins and not to judge my brother,***
***for You are blessed from all ages to all ages. Amen.***
