# The Aaronic Blessing

This blessing was given to Moses on Mount Sinai for Aaron and his sons to speak over the people of Israel. It is recorded in the Book of Numbers and remains among the oldest liturgical texts still in active use. For three thousand years it has been spoken in synagogues and churches, at weddings and funerals, over children and the dying. The Hebrew verbs are in the singular — the blessing addresses each person individually, even when spoken over a congregation. It moves from light to presence to peace, each line expanding outward from the one before. In Jewish tradition, the kohanim (descendants of Aaron) chant it with hands raised and fingers spread in a prescribed form. Christians conclude it with a Trinitarian formula or let it stand as received. It is perhaps the most widely prayed blessing in human history.

---

***The Lord bless you and keep you;***
***the Lord make his face to shine upon you and be gracious to you;***
***the Lord lift up his countenance upon you and give you peace.***
