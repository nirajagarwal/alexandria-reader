# Gatha for Waking

This gatha comes from the Vietnamese Zen tradition, transmitted through Thich Nhat Hanh and practiced in mindfulness communities worldwide. A gatha is a short verse recited to bring awareness to daily activities. This one is traditionally said upon waking, before the feet touch the floor. It marks the threshold between sleep and day, turning the ordinary moment of rising into an act of attention. The practice is simple: wake, pause, speak these words silently or aloud, then stand.

---

***Waking up this morning, I smile.***
***Twenty-four brand new hours are before me.***
***I vow to live fully in each moment***
***and to look at all beings with eyes of compassion.***
