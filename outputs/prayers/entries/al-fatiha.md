# Al-Fatiha

The Opening. The first surah of the Qur'an, recited in every unit of the Muslim daily prayers — at least seventeen times each day for the observant. It is to Islam what the Lord's Prayer is to Christianity: the essential prayer, the one that shapes all others. Revealed to Muhammad in Mecca in the earliest years of his prophetic ministry, it has been called "the Mother of the Book," containing within its seven verses the whole of the Qur'anic message. Muslims stand to recite it, palms open before them, then draw their hands over their faces as they finish — a gesture of receiving blessing. It addresses God directly, acknowledges His sovereignty and mercy, asks for guidance, and names the straight path as distinct from the paths of those who have gone astray. Brief, comprehensive, returning again and again in the rhythm of prayer.

---

***In the name of God, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful***

***Praise be to God, Lord of all the worlds***
***The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful***
***Master of the Day of Judgment***

***You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help***

***Guide us to the straight path—***
***The path of those You have blessed,***
***Not of those who have earned Your anger,***
***Nor of those who have gone astray***
