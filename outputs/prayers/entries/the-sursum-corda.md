# The Sursum Corda

This dialogue has opened Christian liturgies since at least the third century, perhaps earlier. It appears in the Apostolic Tradition attributed to Hippolytus of Rome and in every major liturgical family — Latin, Byzantine, Coptic, Syrian. The priest or minister speaks; the congregation responds. It is sung or said at the threshold of the Eucharistic prayer, the moment when the community gathers its attention upward. "Sursum corda" — lift up your hearts. Not lift up your thoughts or your hopes, but your hearts, the whole center of yourself. The response is immediate, declarative: we have. Already they are lifted, already turned toward what is coming.

---

***Lift up your hearts.***

***We lift them up to the Lord.***

***Let us give thanks to the Lord our God.***

***It is right to give him thanks and praise.***
