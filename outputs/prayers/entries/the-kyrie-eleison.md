# The Kyrie Eleison

One of the oldest continuous prayers in Christian worship, emerging from the earliest centuries of the church and still prayed today in liturgies across traditions. The Greek words — *Kyrie eleison, Christe eleison* — have been sung and spoken without translation for two millennia, carried through Latin masses and Orthodox services, through medieval plainsong and Bach's settings, through the mouths of peasants and emperors. A cry for mercy. Three iterations: to the Father, to the Son, returning to the Father. In some traditions it is sung nine times, in others three. It asks for what cannot be earned, only given.
