# For All That Has Been

Dag Hammarskjöld wrote this in his private journal sometime in the 1950s. It was found after his death in 1961, among the pages that would be published as *Markings*. He was then Secretary-General of the United Nations, carrying the weight of a fractured world, yet this entry is spare — two lines, a breath of gratitude and readiness. It has since been prayed at funerals, at year's end, at thresholds. The Swedish original holds an even simpler rhythm, but the English has become its own prayer now, spoken by those who find themselves between what was and what will be.

---

***For all that has been — thanks.***
***For all that will be — yes.***
