# The Gloria

The Gloria in excelsis Deo — "Glory to God in the highest" — is one of Christianity's oldest hymns, dating to the fourth century or earlier. It begins with the song the angels sang to shepherds at Christ's birth, then expands into sustained praise of the Trinity. The Eastern church sings it at morning prayer. The Western church places it in the Sunday Mass, just after the opening rites — a threshold between gathering and word, the community's voice lifted before silence falls for Scripture. It is sung, not said. The music carries what words alone cannot: the Gloria is doxology, the creature's response to being met by glory.

---

***Glory to God in the highest,***
***and on earth peace to people of good will.***

***We praise you,***
***we bless you,***
***we adore you,***
***we glorify you,***
***we give you thanks for your great glory,***
***Lord God, heavenly King,***
***O God, almighty Father.***

***Lord Jesus Christ, Only Begotten Son,***
***Lord God, Lamb of God, Son of the Father,***
***you take away the sins of the world,***
***have mercy on us;***
***you take away the sins of the world,***
***receive our prayer;***
***you are seated at the right hand of the Father,***
***have mercy on us.***

***For you alone are the Holy One,***
***you alone are the Lord,***
***you alone are the Most High,***
***Jesus Christ,***
***with the Holy Spirit,***
***in the glory of God the Father.***
***Amen.***
