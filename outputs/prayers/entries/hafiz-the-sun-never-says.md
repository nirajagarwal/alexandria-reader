# The Sun Never Says

This comes from Hafiz, the 14th-century Persian Sufi poet whose name means "one who has memorized the Quran." He wrote in Shiraz, and his tomb there remains a place of pilgrimage. In Sufi tradition, the divine is not distant but radiantly present — the lover who waits in everything. This short poem is both observation and aspiration: how to love as the sun loves, without keeping account.

---

***Even***
***After***
***All this time***
***The sun never says to the earth,***

***"You owe***
***Me."***

***Look***
***What happens***
***With a love like that,***
***It lights the***
***Whole***
***Sky.***
