# Collect for Purity

This prayer opens Morning Prayer in the Book of Common Prayer, though its roots reach back to the medieval Sarum Rite of pre-Reformation England. Thomas Cranmer translated and adapted it in 1549, giving it the measured cadence that would shape Anglican worship for centuries. It is prayed at the threshold of liturgy — a clearing of inner space, an acknowledgment that true prayer requires a kind of seeing available only to God. The worshiper asks to be made transparent, to have desire itself aligned with what is holy. It is both confession and petition: we cannot know ourselves rightly without help, and we cannot pray rightly without being known.

---

***Almighty God, unto whom all hearts are open, all desires known, and from whom no secrets are hid: Cleanse the thoughts of our hearts by the inspiration of thy Holy Spirit, that we may perfectly love thee, and worthily magnify thy holy Name; through Christ our Lord. Amen.***
