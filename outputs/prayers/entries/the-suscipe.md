# The Suscipe

Ignatius of Loyola wrote this prayer near the end of his *Spiritual Exercises*, composed in the 1520s and 30s. It comes after weeks of meditation and discernment, at the point where the one praying has considered God's gifts and desires to respond. "Suscipe" is Latin for "receive" or "take." This is a prayer of total offering — not dramatic renunciation but quiet placement of everything back into the hands from which it came. Jesuits and those who make the Exercises pray it as an act of surrender, though the word itself never appears. It names what is given: freedom, memory, understanding, will. What remains is love and grace, which the prayer names as enough.

---

***Take, Lord, and receive all my liberty,***
***my memory, my understanding,***
***and my entire will,***
***all I have and call my own.***

***You have given all to me.***
***To you, Lord, I return it.***

***Everything is yours; do with it what you will.***
***Give me only your love and your grace.***
***That is enough for me.***
