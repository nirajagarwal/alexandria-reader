# My Lord God, I Have No Idea

Thomas Merton, Cistercian monk and contemplative writer, composed this prayer in the mid-twentieth century. It appears in his book *Thoughts in Solitary Confinement* and has become one of the most widely prayed expressions of faith amid uncertainty. Merton wrote it from Gethsemani Abbey in Kentucky, where he lived in community and solitude for twenty-seven years. The prayer speaks to those who walk by trust rather than sight — who cannot see the path clearly but continue forward. It is prayed by those who find honesty more sacred than certainty.

---

***My Lord God, I have no idea where I am going.***
***I do not see the road ahead of me.***
***I cannot know for certain where it will end.***
***Nor do I really know myself,***
***and the fact that I think I am following your will***
***does not mean that I am actually doing so.***

***But I believe that the desire to please you***
***does in fact please you.***
***And I hope I have that desire in all that I am doing.***
***I hope that I will never do anything apart from that desire.***
***And I know that if I do this you will lead me by the right road,***
***though I may know nothing about it.***

***Therefore I will trust you always***
***though I may seem to be lost and in the shadow of death.***
***I will not fear, for you are ever with me,***
***and you will never leave me to face my perils alone.***
