# The Shanti Mantra

From the Upanishads, sacred texts of ancient India composed between 800 and 200 BCE, this prayer has been chanted for millennia at the opening and closing of sacred recitation. It is a petition for peace threefold: peace in the cosmos, peace in the world around us, peace within. The repetition of *shanti* — peace — is itself the prayer's rhythm, the sound becoming what it names. It is chanted in Sanskrit, the sound of the words carrying as much weight as their meaning.

---

***Om***
***Sahana vavatu***
***Sahanau bhunaktu***
***Saha viryam karavavahai***
***Tejasvi navadhitamastu***
***Ma vidvishavahai***

***Om shanti shanti shanti***

---

***May we be protected together***
***May we be nourished together***
***May we work together with great energy***
***May our study be enlightening***
***May we not hate each other***

***Om peace peace peace***
