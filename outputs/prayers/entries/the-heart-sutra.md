# The Heart Sutra

The *Prajñāpāramitā Hṛdaya*, the "Heart of the Perfection of Wisdom," is among the most recited texts in Mahayana Buddhism. Emerging sometime in the early centuries of the Common Era, it distills the vast *Prajñāpāramitā* literature into 260 Sanskrit characters. Monks and laypeople chant it daily in temples across East Asia — in Sanskrit, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Tibetan. It is chanted for clarity, for protection, for awakening. The sutra speaks of emptiness (*śūnyatā*) — not as void but as the true nature of all things, beyond grasping. Avalokiteśvara, the Bodhisattva of Compassion, speaks to Śāriputra, the disciple of wisdom, and the wisdom turns out to be the letting-go of all categories, all fear, all clinging.

---

***Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, practicing deep Prajñāpāramitā,***
***clearly saw that all five skandhas are empty, transforming all suffering and distress.***

***Śāriputra, form is no other than emptiness, emptiness no other than form;***
***form is exactly emptiness, emptiness exactly form;***
***sensation, perception, reaction, and consciousness are also like this.***

***Śāriputra, all things are marked by emptiness—***
***not born, not destroyed; not stained, not pure; not increasing, not decreasing.***

***Therefore in emptiness there is no form, no sensation, perception, reaction, consciousness;***
***no eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, mind;***
***no color, sound, smell, taste, touch, object of mind;***
***no realm of sight, no realm of consciousness;***
***no ignorance and no end to ignorance,***
***no old age and death, and no end to old age and death;***
***no suffering, no cause of suffering, no extinguishing, no path;***
***no wisdom and no gain. No gain—and thus***

***Bodhisattvas live this Prajñāpāramitā***
***with no hindrance of mind. No hindrance, therefore no fear.***
***Far beyond all delusion, Nirvana is already here.***

***All Buddhas past, present, and future live this Prajñāpāramitā***
***and realize perfect, complete awakening.***

***Therefore know that Prajñāpāramitā is***
***the great mantra, the vivid mantra,***
***the unsurpassed mantra, the supreme mantra,***
***which completely removes all suffering.***
***This is truth, not deception.***

***Therefore set forth the Prajñāpāramitā mantra,***
***set forth this mantra and proclaim:***

***Gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā!***
***Gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā!***
***Gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā!***
