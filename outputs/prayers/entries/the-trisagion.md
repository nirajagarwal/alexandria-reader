# The Trisagion

The Trisagion — "thrice holy" — has been sung in Eastern Christian liturgy since at least the fifth century. In the Divine Liturgy of the Eastern Orthodox Church, it is chanted as the Little Entrance procession moves through the nave, the congregation joining their voices with the angels who stand before God's throne. The prayer appears in Isaiah's vision and again in Revelation — the seraphim crying out ceaselessly. To pray it is to join that chorus, acknowledging the holiness that precedes all speech and outlasts all silence. It is sung in Greek, in Slavonic, in Arabic, in every language where the Eastern churches pray.
