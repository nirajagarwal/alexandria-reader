# Prayer of Rabi'a al-Adawiyya

Rabi'a al-Adawiyya lived in eighth-century Basra, a freed slave who became one of Sufism's most revered voices. She is remembered for teaching that love of God should be pure — not motivated by fear of hell or hope of paradise, but for God's own sake. This prayer, transmitted through oral tradition and preserved in the writings of later Sufis, expresses that vision. It asks for liberation from the bargaining that can enter even devotion. She prayed it alone, as one standing before the Beloved with empty hands.

---

***O my Lord,***
***if I worship You from fear of Hell, burn me in Hell,***
***and if I worship You from hope of Paradise, exclude me from Paradise.***

***But if I worship You for Your own sake,***
***do not withhold from me Your Eternal Beauty.***
