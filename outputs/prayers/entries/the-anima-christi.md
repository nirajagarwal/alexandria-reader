# The Anima Christi

This prayer emerged in the early fourteenth century, though its author remains unknown. By the time Ignatius of Loyola placed it at the beginning of his *Spiritual Exercises* in the sixteenth century, it had already been prayed widely across the Western church. It is a prayer of approach and reception, asking that Christ would enter every faculty — body and soul, wound and water, ear and heart. It moves from petition to refuge, from intimacy to defense, ending with a plea not to be separated. Traditionally prayed after communion, though it belongs wherever the soul reaches toward presence.

---

***Soul of Christ, sanctify me***
***Body of Christ, save me***
***Blood of Christ, inebriate me***
***Water from the side of Christ, wash me***
***Passion of Christ, strengthen me***
***O good Jesus, hear me***
***Within Thy wounds hide me***
***Suffer me not to be separated from Thee***
***From the malicious enemy defend me***
***In the hour of my death call me***
***And bid me come unto Thee***
***That with Thy saints I may praise Thee***
***Forever and ever. Amen.***
