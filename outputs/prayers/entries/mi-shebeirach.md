# Mi Shebeirach

*Mi Shebeirach* — "May the One who blessed" — is the opening of a Hebrew prayer for healing that has been recited in Jewish communities for centuries. Traditionally chanted in synagogue when the Torah is read, it calls upon the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, of Sarah, Rebecca, Rachel, and Leah, to bless those who are ill. The prayer names the person needing healing, using their Hebrew name and their mother's name, linking them to their lineage and to the covenant. It asks for *refuah shleimah* — complete healing, both of body and spirit. In recent decades, this prayer has moved beyond synagogue walls, sung at bedsides and in vigils, reaching toward the mystery of healing that comes from beyond our hands.

---

***Mi shebeirach avoteinu v'imoteinu,***
***Avraham, Yitzchak, v'Yaakov,***
***Sarah, Rivkah, Rachel, v'Leah,***
***hu y'vareich et [name] ben/bat [mother's name].***

***HaKadosh Baruch Hu yimalei rachamim alav/aleha,***
***l'hachalimo ul'rapoto, ul'hachaziko, ul'hachayoto.***
***V'yishlach lo/lah m'heirah r'fuah shleimah,***
***r'fuah hanefesh ur'fuah haguf,***
***b'toch sh'ar cholei Yisrael v'cholei yoshvei teiveil,***
***hashta ba'agala uvizman kariv. V'nomar: Amen.***

***May the One who blessed our ancestors —***
***Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob,***
***Sarah, Rebecca, Rachel, and Leah —***
***bless and heal [name] son/daughter of [mother's name].***

***May the Holy One, blessed be He, overflow with compassion upon him/her,***
***to restore him/her, to heal him/her, to strengthen him/her, and to revive him/her.***
***And may He send him/her speedily a complete recovery,***
***a healing of soul and a healing of body,***
***together with all the ill of Israel and all who dwell on earth,***
***swiftly and soon. And let us say: Amen.***
