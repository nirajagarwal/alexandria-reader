# The Sanctus

The Sanctus, or "Holy, Holy, Holy," rises from Isaiah's vision of the seraphim crying out before the throne of God. By the fourth century, it had entered Christian liturgy, joining heaven's worship to the Eucharistic prayer. It is sung or said in nearly every tradition that celebrates the Mass or Divine Liturgy — Catholic, Orthodox, Anglican, Lutheran. The congregation joins the angels in the recognition that holiness saturates everything, that heaven and earth are already full of God's glory, whether we have eyes to see it or not. The prayer does not ask. It proclaims what is.

---

***Holy, holy, holy, Lord God of hosts,***
***heaven and earth are full of thy glory.***
***Glory be to thee, O Lord most high.***

***Blessed is he that cometh in the name of the Lord.***
***Hosanna in the highest.***
