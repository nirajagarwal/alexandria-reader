# Let Nothing Disturb You

Teresa of Ávila, the sixteenth-century Spanish Carmelite, left this prayer in a breviary shortly before her death in 1582. She had reformed her order, founded seventeen convents, written works of mystical theology that would make her a Doctor of the Church. She died in poverty, her body worn from decades of travel and labor. These lines were found in her handwriting — a prayer she had carried, a distillation of what she had learned about God's sufficiency through all turbulence.

It is prayed by those seeking stillness in the midst of upheaval, by those holding fast when everything shifts. The Spanish original has a rhythm that steadies the breath: *Nada te turbe, nada te espante*.

---

***Let nothing disturb you,***
***Let nothing frighten you,***
***All things are passing away:***
***God never changes.***
***Patience obtains all things.***
***Whoever has God lacks nothing;***
***God alone suffices.***
