# The Jesus Prayer

In the Orthodox tradition of the Christian East, this prayer has been repeated continuously for more than a millennium — whispered, chanted, synchronized with breath, woven into the rhythm of a day or a life. Monks on Mount Athos pray it thousands of times daily. Laypeople carry it through cities, through grief, through joy. It is both the simplest prayer and the most persistent. The words ask for mercy. The practice asks for transformation — the prayer descending from the lips to the mind to the heart, becoming as involuntary as breathing.

---

***Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me, a sinner.***
