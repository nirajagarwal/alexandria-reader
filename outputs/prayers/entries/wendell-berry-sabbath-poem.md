# Sabbath Poem VI

Wendell Berry wrote a series of Sabbath poems beginning in 1979, walking the woods near his Kentucky farm on Sundays. He left his work behind — the farm, the page, the world's demands — and entered what he called "the peace of wild things." These poems are meditations, prayers of attention. They ask nothing, explain nothing. They rest in what is given.

This is the sixth poem from 1979. It is prayed by walking, by stopping, by seeing what is there when you cease to want anything from it.

---

***Within the circles of our lives***
***we dance the circles of the years,***
***the circles of the seasons***
***within the circles of the years,***
***the cycles of the moon***
***within the circles of the seasons,***
***the circles of our reasons***
***within the cycles of the moon.***

***Again, again we come and go,***
***changed, changing. Hands***
***join, unjoin in love and fear,***
***grief and joy. The circles turn,***
***each giving into each, into all.***
***Only music keeps us here,***

***each by all the others held.***
***In the hold of present time,***
***the circle of the year, the swing***
***of rhythms, we are come to stay.***
***And we are part of every place***
***we go. We are returned.***

***And in the midst of time,***
***wherever we may be,***
***love moves among us,***
***asking for itself.***
***And we, in every place, love must***
***pay heed and serve at once,***
***in all things serve—in the work***
***of the world, the duties of the day,***
***the great offices and the small.***

***We must serve in the passing moments,***
***and we must serve in the lasting years,***
***and we must serve in the peace***
***and in the strife—remembering***
***and remembering, and remembering.***
***And we must serve one another,***
***loving each other,***
***as we are loved by what is past,***
***as we are loved by the ancient love***
***that calls us to our present life.***
