# The Refuges

In the Theravada and Mahayana traditions, the Three Refuges — also called the Triple Gem — form the fundamental declaration of Buddhist practice. These lines, recited in Pali or the practitioner's own language, mark the formal entry into Buddhist life and are repeated daily by monks, nuns, and laypeople. They are spoken at the beginning of ceremonies, before meditation, as a recommitment. To take refuge is to acknowledge what offers genuine shelter in a world of impermanence: the Buddha (the awakened teacher), the Dharma (the teachings and the truth they point to), and the Sangha (the community of practitioners). The formula is simple, ancient, and central.

---

***Buddham saranam gacchami***
***Dhammam saranam gacchami***
***Sangham saranam gacchami***

***I go to the Buddha for refuge***
***I go to the Dharma for refuge***
***I go to the Sangha for refuge***
