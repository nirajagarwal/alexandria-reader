# Lead Me from the Unreal

This prayer comes from the Brihadaranyaka Upanishad, one of the oldest Upanishads, composed in Sanskrit sometime between the 8th and 6th century BCE. It belongs to the Vedantic tradition of Hindu philosophy and spirituality. The prayer is known as the Pavamana Mantra — the purification mantra — and has been chanted for millennia as part of contemplative practice and ritual worship. It moves through three progressions: from illusion to truth, from darkness to light, from death to immortality. The prayer asks not for external deliverance but for transformation of perception, that what is false might fall away and what is real might be seen.

---

***Lead me from the unreal to the real.***
***Lead me from darkness to light.***
***Lead me from death to immortality.***
