# The Gayatri Mantra

One of the oldest prayers in continuous use, drawn from the Rig Veda and traditionally attributed to the sage Vishvamitra. Sanskrit-speaking Hindus have chanted these lines for more than three thousand years — at dawn, at temple, in private devotion. It is addressed to Savitar, the solar deity, but reaches toward the divine light that illumines all consciousness. Brahmins recite it as part of daily practice. Others take it up as a universal prayer, asking that the mind be guided toward truth. It is twenty-four syllables in Sanskrit, metrical and exact, meant to be spoken aloud.

---

***Om bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ***
***tat savitur vareṇyam***
***bhargo devasya dhīmahi***
***dhiyo yo naḥ pracodayāt***

***We meditate on the glory of the Creator***
***who has created the universe***
***who is worthy of worship***
***who is the embodiment of knowledge and light***
***who is the remover of all sin and ignorance.***
***May he enlighten our intellect.***
