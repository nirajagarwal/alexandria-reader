# All Shall Be Well

In May 1373, Julian of Norwich, an anchorite in her cell beside St. Julian's Church, received sixteen visions during a severe illness. She spent the next twenty years contemplating what she had seen. From those showings came this assurance, spoken to her by Christ — words that have steadied souls through plague, war, doubt, and ordinary darkness for six centuries. Julian records them simply, as they were given. They are not argument or explanation. They are promise.

---

***All shall be well,***
***and all shall be well,***
***and all manner of thing shall be well.***
